Mandarins

Mandarins, by Ryūnosuke Akutagawa is inherently about what it’s like to be human - confused, adapting, struggling to understand other people and a changing world. His stories depict minds trying to navigate life alongside other minds.

Our life’s work is greater intelligence rooted in human cognition - sample efficient, alignable, built to navigate life with us. Artificial intelligence that lives in a human world, alongside and synergistically with human intelligence.1

We want superintelligence to understand Mandarins because it shares the struggle.
