Mandarins

Mandarins, by Ryūnosuke Akutagawa, is inherently about what it’s like to be human. His stories depict minds trying to navigate life alongside other minds, struggling to understand other people and a changing world.

Humans learn from remarkably little. We build rich models of the world and other minds. We generalize to situations we've never seen. Our architecture demonstrates core tenets of cognition. And what current AI paradigms lack.

Our focus is advancing artificial intelligence that lives in a human world, alongside and synergistically with human intelligence.1

We want superintelligence to understand Mandarins because it shares our struggle.
